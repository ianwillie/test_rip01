SMG Glasgow News
“1st June 77 “The committee of SMG Glasgow intent on keeping in touch with its members and providing greater involvement in decision-making, will be publishing this news-sheet each month. With the aim of providing a more localised form of information and comments, this is no way intended as a substitute for SMG News which you will still receive.”

“EXCLUSIVE:
Following after the Daily Mail's exhaustive revelations concerning British Layland, we have uncovered startling details of a cash fund set up by Glasgow SMG. The existence of this fund, for a long time suspected, has now been confirmed by two of Glasgow's part time workers who have been persuaded to reveal all. Not a pretty sight. In a 'NEWS' exclusive we can now detail the full workings and history of this hitherto unpublished episode.

Secretly discussed in March of this year, the final details of the fund were completed in April.

One of the two main conspirators in setting up the fund (codenames D.D. and P.G.) was knocked down in mysterious circumstances while s trolling at night on the M8. The vehicle concerned was later discovered to be associated with the Company now under investigation by the Prime Minister, British Leyland.

The fund operates in this manner. Members of SMG (codenamed 'sponsors') agree to pay, usuall from secret bank accounts but sometimes in used notes, various sums of money per month for a period of one year. In return they receive 'Sponsor Cards' entitling them to free access to all SMG events.

Money from this fund is used to improve the facilities, modernise and redecorate the club in Sauchiehall St. which the Scottish Daily Express Editor, Charles Graham, so fearlessly exposed in February.

Now in its third month of operation the total sum of £240 has been received by the operators of this fund. Now there are twenty-five regular Sponsors donating £122.50 per month to SMG.

When asked for their comments on the existence of this secret fund, the Committee stated:
“We are indebted to all those helping us through this system and allowing the work to go ahead on the premises.”

SHADOWS SIEGE
Shadows Discotheque at the Western Hotel was invaded by a group of teenage thugs recently. This gang, mainly female and numbering around twelve, forced an entry and assaulted members and staff, hurling abuse and insults before being removed. The police were not involved.
This form of provocation and unnecessary violence is no stranger to Glasgow although rare at organised gay events. It is to be hoped that such incidents do not become common place and that this type of violence will not stop people from enjoying themselves at the Western Hotel in future.

COMMUNITY HOUSE
It is with much regret that we learn of the forthcoming closure of Community House. It is to be hoped that the valuable work of the House, as both a Day Centre and evening Meeting Place can be continued in Glasgow. SMG Glasgow expresses it heartfelt thanks (cont.)

(cont.) and its gratitude to the staff and Members of the Iona Community for their invaluable help and assistance. SMG Glasgow is fortunate in having its own premises to move into but it is important to remember that there are many groups using Community House who do not have such facilities. The disappearance of Community House after 33 years of devoted and practical social work, providing for those in need is a tragic loss to Glasgow's Community.

Gay Handicapped Group
Those who are physically handicapped in any way very often have their full human rights restrained and their access to facilities reduced through thoughtlessness and lack of proper planning. It is sufficient to show only an intent and willingness to help, when asked, but a more positive attitude is required when offering assistance.

Glasgow SMG is prepared to provide all the help and facilities at its disposal in the formation of a Gay Handicapped Group. Should any of you know of anyone who is interested, whether disabled or not, in assisting with such a group then please contact Paul gale at SMG Glasgow, or ring the Advisory service, tel. 041-332-3382, on a Monday evening.

OPEN HOUSE
The new premises at 534, Sauchiehall St. were the scene of an Open House meeting on the evening of the 24th. May. Around forty members came along to inspect the premises and see the work being undertaken. To those not prepared to poke around behind the plasterboard paneling few dramatic changes or improvements were to be noted. However, considerable work has been carried out towards the opening date in August. The plumbing is nearly complete, despite the temporary setback which allowed the Charing Cross Hotel to advertise for a short period running water in EVERY room.

Our resident 'Live Wire' (Aaagh!), Donald Blaney has nearly completed the electrical installation which will be the envy of Blackpool Illuminations once this intricate task has been completed, the work of renovating the Meeting Room can continue. BS was in attendance and creating a very [column break] busy bar atmosphere with a plentiful supply of drink and the not unexpected packets of crisps. SMG Glasgow would like to thank all those who attended and made it such a successful evening.

Next week the Community Industry Team will move in again to transform the meeting room hopefully within a few weeks. Once the work of lowering the ceiling and demolishing the dividing wall is complete , the remaining work is mainly cosmetic, ie. Plastering, painting and decorating. The closure of Community House makes the completion of this work even more important and it is the intention of the Glasgow Committee to open the premises to the public as soon as possible.

RATES RELIEF
The Glasgow SMG premises at S St have been awarded discretionary Rates Relief by Glasgow District Coulcil. This is due to the invaluable counselling work carried out by the premises by the Glasgow Gay Advisory Service under the guidance of Ian Millar. Most of us realise the great value of the work carried out by the Advisory Service but this is recognition from an unexpected source and worth around £2000 per year. Carry on the good work.
