After meeting (and drinking) in the Toby Jug for several years local gays were surprised to learn that the new proprietor, Mr Danny McDonald has made it clear that he does nto want gays in his bar. The trouble is he cannot identify then unless they are pointed out to him.

Changing attitudes
In the long run changes in the law are instrumental in changing people's thinking.
We have come a long way since the pre-Wollfenden [Report 1964] days but we have stagnated and we have a long way to go. A further change in the law regarding the age of consent (if we have to have an age of consent) is an essential pre-requisite for a more healthy climate in sexual matters.

Contributed ty a Paisley & District member.

STRATHCLYDE POLICE QUESTION GAY MEN IN GLASGOW

We have heard that at least 10 men have been questioned in Glasgow recently regarding under age sexual activity. The police were alleged to have told one man that he would be prosecuted if he had a relationship with any male person up to his 21st birthday. They also informed the man's employer who has subsequently brought pressure on him to resign.
